<section id="traffic-incident-prediction" class="project">
## Network-wide Traffic Incident Prediction
<p class="meta">Transportation Research Part C, 2023</p>
<div class="tags"><span class="tag">Graph Neural Networks</span><span class="tag">Data Fusion</span><span class="tag">Real-time Prediction</span></div>

- Built MSGNN, a multi-structured graph neural network for predicting traffic incident occurrence across large road networks.
- Represented each sub-area as graph inputs so the model could learn from road topology and heterogeneous data sources.
- Evaluated across Brisbane and Gold Coast networks at 15-minute and 60-minute prediction horizons.
- Published as open-access research in Transportation Research Part C.

<p class="links"><a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2023.104354">DOI</a> <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0968090X23003443">Open article</a></p>

## Multimodal Incident Forecasting with M2NN
<p class="meta">Transportation Research Part C, 2026</p>
<div class="tags"><span class="tag">Multimodal AI</span><span class="tag">GNN</span><span class="tag">CNN</span><span class="tag">RNN</span></div>

- Developed a multi-view, multi-task neural network that combines traffic sensor data with congestion heatmap imagery.
- Jointly modelled link-level and area-level incident probabilities to improve consistency across prediction targets.
- Designed for scalable network-wide forecasting while retaining customisable sub-area definitions.
- Published as open-access research in Transportation Research Part C.

<p class="links"><a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.trc.2025.105458">DOI</a> <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0968090X25004620">Open article</a></p>
